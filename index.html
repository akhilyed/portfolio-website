Notable Projects

Game Jam: Roboting

A Game my team and I made for A Game Jam that was held during school. The theme of the competition was "Better Together", and we made a local multiplayer game that involves platforming and avoiding obstacles until you reach the end. We wanted to create a game that uses obstacles that are impossible to cross without two players, which we did with higher platforms and long gaps where you need to jump on top of each other to cross. We also wanted to add obstacles that one player could cross, but the other can't, and vice versa, which we did through our colored spikes. If the spikes were red, then only the red player could cross, and blue for the blue player. Most debugging involved making sure all the assets showed and the logic for movement also worked. One challenge we faced was with the players jumping on top of each other, as when they did, it would be really hard to stay in place, since you need to be on top to complete some of the platforming. We solved this issue by fixing the position of the player on top to always be over the bottom player until they hit the jump button, in which they could move left or right again. We used HTML and CSS for the website and Unity for the game.

Digicure

An app I made that allows the user to browse a database and easily attain information on a large number of OTC medicines, including what it treats, ingredients, brand names, and side effects. My partner and I wanted to create an app that allows people to safely purchase medicine without worrying about the dangers of getting the wrong medicine or not knowing the dangerous side effects it may have. We wanted to first format all of the data so that our displayer could understand it, and we also wanted to add a chatbot that would allow users to get information even more easily, which we did. We had to debug missing data as well as the chatbot, as it would sometimes break down. A challenge we had was actually finding data that we could use, as we wanted to include over thousands of different types of medicines, and most of the data we found was either too small or missing info. In the end, we were able to find a dataset with an extensive amount of medicines from DailyMed, a site made by the National Library of Medicine. It uses Python as well as the Tkinter library for GUI, XML.etree for formatting data and selection, and genai for adding the chatbot.

AI Project: AI Guessing Word Game

An AI game that I made for my AI project, which allows the user to input text that the AI will use to try to guess the secret word it doesn't know. You can choose a difficulty, and it also uses the wonderwords library to generate a seemingly infinite amount of random words to use. I wanted to see how well AI could predict words using human inputs from the user, which makes it harder for the AI since it's only used to the perfect relations it has to words. For the design, I wanted to make it look like the chatbots we see, like ChatGPT or Gemini. I continued to fix the intelligence of AI as time went on, and a specific challenge I had was getting a seemingly random word, as other solutions would require me to hard-code a list of words. By using the wonderwords library, I was able to overcome this. It uses Python, wonderwords, numpy for math, streamlit for hosting and web framework, and sentence_transformers for converting words to math vectors.
